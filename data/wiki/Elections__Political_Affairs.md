# Elections & Political Affairs

## Introduction

Elections are a cornerstone of democratic governance, allowing citizens to select leaders and influence policy. This report compiles information from various declassified documents obtained through the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) to provide an exhaustive overview of elections and political affairs, focusing on the mechanisms, regulations, and political activities surrounding electoral processes [ForgedInTheFire_2008_p22; EACtransBriefBiden_2020_p14; BBGmoRpts_2012-2015_p51].

## Key Facts and Findings

1. **Election Organization and Oversight**: The Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe (OSCE) plays a significant role in international elections, with local entities like the Provisional Election Commission (PEC) and Local Elections Commission (LEC) managing specific regulatory and operational tasks <a href="/pdf/ForgedInTheFire_2008#page=22" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2008, p.22)</a>. The OSCE's involvement underscores the importance of oversight in maintaining electoral integrity across borders <a href="/pdf/BBGmoRpts_2012-2015#page=51" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2012-2015, p.51)</a>.

2. **U.S. Election Administration**: The U.S. election process is decentralized, with each state and territory administering elections independently. This results in a wide variety of practices, making the dissemination of accurate information crucial but resource-intensive <a href="/pdf/EACtransBriefBiden_2020#page=14" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2020, p.14)</a>. The Federal Election Commission (FEC) administers and enforces the Federal Election Campaign Act (FECA), ensuring transparency in campaign finance <a href="/pdf/ClosDocs15FECoigInvs_2008-2015u#page=80" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2008-2015u, p.80)</a>.

3. **Political Activities and Restrictions**: Federal employees are subject to various restrictions regarding political activities, including prohibitions on campaigning for partisan elections while on duty <a href="/pdf/OSCadvOpinHatchAct_2017-2019#page=115" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2017-2019, p.115)</a>. The Hatch Act is a critical piece of legislation governing political activities of federal employees, ensuring that government resources are not used for partisan purposes <a href="/pdf/OCSformalHatchActAOWL_Jan-Jun2021#page=57" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #Jan-Jun2021, p.57)</a>.

4. **Foreign Involvement and Security**: There are ongoing concerns about foreign interference in U.S. elections, as highlighted by intelligence and security discussions <a href="/pdf/ODNIfoiaLogs_2017-2020#page=50" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2017-2020, p.50)</a>. The Department of Homeland Security and other agencies continue to monitor and address potential threats to election integrity, emphasizing the need for vigilance against foreign interference <a href="/documents/CISAminAgendasGFS-EIS_2019-2020_chunk13" class="citation" target="_blank">[CISAminAgendasGFS-EIS_2019-2020_chunk13]</a>.

5. **Impact of Political Cycles**: Political cycles in partner nations can affect their military operations and readiness, illustrating the intersection of military and electoral timelines <a href="/pdf/TipOfTheSpear_2009#page=424" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2009, p.424)</a>.

## Historical Context

### U.S. Elections and Political Developments

- The Federal Election Campaign Act (FECA) provides the legal framework for campaign finance in the United States, with the Federal Election Commission (FEC) administering and enforcing these regulations <a href="/pdf/ClosDocs15FECoigInvs_2008-2015u#page=80" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2008-2015u, p.80)</a>.
- Historical elections, such as the 1912 presidential election, have marked significant political shifts, often prompted by public dissatisfaction with entrenched power structures <a href="/pdf/DallasFedResBkUponAStrongFoundation1992#page=16" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #DallasFedResBkUponAStrongFoundation1992, p.16)</a>.

### International Elections and Observations

- The OSCE's involvement in international elections underscores the importance of oversight in maintaining electoral integrity across borders <a href="/pdf/ForgedInTheFire_2008#page=22" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2008, p.22)</a>.
- Programs like the U.S. Election Program by IFES offer international observers insights into the U.S. electoral process, promoting transparency and understanding <a href="/pdf/BBGmoRpts_2012-2015#page=51" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #2012-2015, p.51)</a>.

## Political Activities and Legal Framework

### Restrictions and Allowances

- Federal employees are generally allowed to participate in nonpartisan activities but face restrictions on partisan political activities, including campaigning and using their official authority to influence election outcomes <a href="/pdf/TVAprinPracManual_1994#page=118" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #1994, p.118)</a>. The Hatch Act ensures that political activities do not compromise the neutrality of federal employees <a href="/pdf/OCSformalHatchActAOWL_Jan-Jun2021#page=57" class="citation" target="_blank">(Doc #Jan-Jun2021, p.57)</a>.

### Election Security and Foreign Interference

- The Department of Homeland Security and other agencies continue to monitor and address potential threats to election integrity, emphasizing the need for vigilance against foreign interference [CISAminAgendasGFS-EIS_2019-2020_chunk13; ODNIfoiaLogs_2017-2020_p50].

## Conclusion

The process of conducting elections involves a complex interplay of legal frameworks, administrative practices, and security considerations. The decentralized nature of U.S. elections requires careful coordination and communication to maintain transparency and trust in the electoral process. Internationally, organizations like the OSCE play a vital role in promoting electoral integrity, while domestic policies like the Hatch Act ensure that political activities do not compromise the neutrality of federal employees.

By examining the declassified documents, this report highlights the multifaceted nature of elections and political affairs, providing insights into both historical and contemporary electoral practices and challenges.
